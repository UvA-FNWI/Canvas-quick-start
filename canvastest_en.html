Canvas quick start

Faculty of Science

This pages gives an overview of the most importants components of a Canvas course.

Home

The home page of the course can contain general information about the course. The most recent Announcements are shown at the top of the page.

Here you also find the Student view button, which you can use to view the course as it will be seen by a student.

Modules

Modules provide structure to the course, grouping material e.g. thematically or chonologically. They can contain various types of content:

Content pages Pages containing text, files or other materials
Files Any type of file uploaded in Canvas
Assignments (Hand-in) exercises
Discussions Discussion forum for students and teachers
External URLs Links to pages outside the course
External tools External applications, such as Turnitin

Within a module there is no room for explanations; pages can be used for this, where you can also easily refer to other content.

Assignments and grades

Everything that has a grade or deadline (including an exam) is an Assignment in Canvas. These can offer online submission or take the form of a Quiz. Assignments can also be linked to Rubrics.

The Grades option allows you to register and publish grades. Digital assignments can be graded and commented on in the SpeedGrader.

Course navigation

Most navigation options are hidden for students by default; their contents can be made available via modules or pages. Navigation options can be turned on in Settings.

The Course manual option is automatically added when the course manual is published in DataNose.

In People you find the enrolled students and teachers. The groups have been imported from DataNose as Sections. Here you can also create Groups for student collaboration.

Publishing content

In Canvas, materials can be set to visible () or hidden () with the Publish option at three distinct levels: content items, modules and the course as a whole. Content can only be found when all 3 levels are set to published.
